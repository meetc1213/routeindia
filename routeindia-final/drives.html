Ek Pahal

The first step towards a better India

Situation of Mahapura

Route India embarked on its journey to clean the litter in India by organizing the cleanup drive Ek Pahal, meaning an initiative, in the village of Mahapura near Jayshree Periwal International School, Jaipur. The village was found to have accumulateed large litter grounds that not only covered useful land, but also catered to breeding grounds for vectors like mosquitos and water pollution. In addition to that, Mahapura has open drains on every road, in front of every house, in front of the gram-panchayat, even in front of the local dispensary! The throbbing problem is that Mahapura doesn't come under any urban authoritites due to which noone collects the ever increasing heaps of litter. With their low incomes and subsistence occupations, the villagers of Mahapura cannot afford the renting of private dumping facilities. Thus, the level of uncollected trash, the lack of motivation among the villagers to collect it, and the lack of means to get rid of the trash inspired us to take the matter in our hands.

Gloves and masks on!

The Route India team along with more than 50 volunteers began the drive to meticulously collect every single piece of plastic and cloth lying around and even collected statistical data keeping up with Route's global approach towards clean up drives. We segregated the trash into different categories like single use plastic, clothes, glassware, rubber materials, and papers & cardboards. We cleaned 3 different sites in the village containing the most trash. Not only were we able to successfully clean the village, but most importantly we succeeded in motivating other fellow villagers to follow the same ROUTE towards a cleaner India. We were soon joined by some volunteers from the village to reach the common goal.

Go green!

Cleaning trash does not outdo the damage it has already caused. Growing more plants and trees not only reduces visual pollution caused by litter, but also makes a positive impact on the whole village ecosystem. Route India understands this and hence took the initiative to plant 10+ trees in Mahapura. We also discussed with the Sarpanch (village govt. head) about the open drains in Mahapura.

Be the change - Be Route!

Though our effort to bring a change was small, the ripple of its consequences cannot be understated. Even the green villages are turning gray with the litter. The gravity of the consequences of ignoring litter are huge and a small initiative can improve a lot!
